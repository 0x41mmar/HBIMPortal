def importTensorDF(df):
    df = df.rename(columns={"Date/Time":"time"})
    df['time'] = pd.to_datetime(df['time'],format="%d/%m/%Y %H:%M:%S")
    df = df.set_index('time')
    df = df.interpolate(method='time',limit=15)
    df = df[~df.index.duplicated(keep='first')]
    df = df.asfreq('1Min',method='pad') #try normalize=True
    df = df.resample('1Min').mean()
    return df

CO2 concentration¶

According to CIBSE, the indoor air quality, a part of indoor environment quality, may be estimated with concentration of contaminants that should not have negative impacts on occupants’ health and performance, such as CO2. ASHRAE standard derived by Pettenkorfer’s studies suggested a maximum CO2 level of 1000 ppm. More recently ASHRAE recommended a difference between indoor and outdoor concentration not more than 700 ppm. According to European standard EN 13779 indoor air quality is considered achieved with less than 400 ppm above the outdoor level, medium quality in the range between 400-600 ppm, moderate from 600 to 1000 ppm and low above 1000 ppm. The concentration of CO2 depends on human activities such as breathing and smoking, high occupancy and limited air change rates for the intake of fresh air. The high concentration of CO2 provokes loss of consciousness (fainting) and limited cognitive performance.
